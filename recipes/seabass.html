Miso and Soy Chilean Sea Bass

The final result of this delicious Chilean Sea Bass

In just about 3 hours, this delicious, melt in your mouth Chilean Sea Bass

(Which, fun fact, isn't actually a real fish) will be ready to eat.

Get ready for the best fish you've ever had the pleasure of enjoying in your life.

Serves four, so why not invite some friends and family over and share the magic with them!

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup sake
  • 1/3 cup mirin (Japanese rice wine)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup miso paste
  • 4 (4 ounce) fillets fresh sea bass, 1 inch thick
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onions

Steps

  1. Whisk together sake, mirin, soy sauce, brown sugar, and miso paste in bowl to make marinade.
  2. Place the sea bass in a large sealable bag and pour marinade over sea bass
  3. Chill in refridgerator for 3-6 hours
  4. Arrange on baking sheet and discard marinade
  5. Preheat oven's broiler and set oven rack about 6 inches from heat source
  6. Prop oven door slightly ajar
  7. Bake sea bass in broiler until it flakes easily with a fork, 7-9 minutes
  8. Sprinkle with chopped green onions and serve!
